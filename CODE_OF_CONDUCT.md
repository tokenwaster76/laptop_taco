# Code of Conduct

## In one sentence

Be the kind of person you'd want in your group chat.

## What that means

- **Be kind.** This is a meme tool. Nobody is curing cancer in this repo. Talk to people like you'd talk to a colleague you respect.
- **Disagree on the work, not on the person.** "This change adds complexity that doesn't earn its lines" is a great review comment. "You don't understand Bash" is not.
- **Take feedback at face value.** If someone says the README confused them, they were confused. Don't argue them out of being confused — fix the README.
- **No harassment.** No discriminatory jokes. No "punching down". No personal attacks. No revenge-tagging.
- **Don't post other people's private information.**
- **English is the working language**, but bad English is fine. The world is bigger than your dictionary.

## If something goes wrong

If you experience or witness behavior that breaks the rules above, open a private security advisory (the same channel as `SECURITY.md` reports):

https://github.com/tokenwaster76/laptop_taco/security/advisories/new

I will read it within 5 business days and take action. Action could be: a private clarification, removal of a comment, blocking a participant from the repo, or simply telling you "yep, that was wrong, sorry." I do not promise infinite patience for bad-faith behavior — I do promise that legitimate reports will get a real answer.

## Scope

This code applies to issues, PRs, discussions, commits, and any other interaction inside this repo. It does not police your behavior outside of it.
